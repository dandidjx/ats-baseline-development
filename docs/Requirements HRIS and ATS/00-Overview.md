# HRIS & ATS System - Overview

## Introduction

An **HRIS** (Human Resources Information System) is a business application used to:
- Support HR teams in managing people-related processes across the employee lifecycle
- Provide structure, visibility, and compliance for workforce data and operations

A **ATS** (Applicant Tracking System) is a core module of an HRIS, focused specifically on **talent acquisition**â€”from job creation to hiring decision.

---

## Core Processes

### Talent Acquisition (ATS)

> "Talent acquisition is the set of business activities an organization uses to identify, attract, evaluate, and hire the right people."

**Scope:**
- Begins when an organization recognizes a need for new talent
- Ends when a hiring decision is made and the selected candidate joins
- Includes planning, market engagement, candidate evaluation, and decision-making

**Key Activities:**
1. **Job Opening Management** - Formalizing hiring needs and aligning stakeholders
2. **Candidate Management** - Building and maintaining long-term talent relationships
3. **Recruitment Pipeline** - Structuring the decision-making journey from interest to hire

## Important Notes

**Hiring Cycle Characteristics:**
- Can be very short (few days) or very long (several months)
- One candidate may apply to multiple jobs
- One job opening may have multiple hired candidates
- Candidate records should remain reusable and traceable across applications

**Business Models:**

1. **End-User Organization (Direct Hiring)**
   - Organization uses ATS for internal hiring
   - Single organization in system
   - Organization is both customer and employer

2. **Headhunter / Recruitment Agency**
   - ATS customer is the headhunter company
   - Organization entity represents client companies
   - Multiple client organizations per system
